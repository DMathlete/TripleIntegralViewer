MA204 — Vector Calculus Visualizers

Interactive 3D tools for Block V: Vector Calculus — line integrals, surfaces, and surface integrals

Scalar Line Integral
$\displaystyle \int_C f\, ds$
Visualize the integral of a scalar function $f(x,y,z)$ along a curve $C$ as the area of a curtain. Includes Riemann sum animation, arc-length mode, and 2D / 3D curve presets.
Vector Line Integral
$\displaystyle \int_C \mathbf{F}\cdot d\mathbf{r}$
Visualize the work done by a vector field $\mathbf{F} = \langle P, Q, R\rangle$ along an oriented curve. Toggle field arrows, tangent projections, a signed work curtain, and a full field grid.
Path Independence & Curl
$\displaystyle \int_C \mathbf{F}\cdot d\mathbf{r} = f(B) - f(A)$
Compare work along two paths with the same endpoints. Visualize scalar curl as a heatmap, auto-detect conservative fields via the component test, and recover a potential function $f$ when it exists.
Green's Theorem
$\displaystyle \oint_{\partial D} P\,dx + Q\,dy = \iint_D (Q_x - P_y)\,dA$
Watch both forms of Green's Theorem verify themselves. Enter any closed curve and field; compare boundary line integrals to interior double integrals for circulation and flux, with a live curl/divergence heatmap and a microcurl cancellation animation.
Parametric Surfaces
$\displaystyle \mathbf{r}(u,v) = \langle x(u,v),\ y(u,v),\ z(u,v)\rangle$
Pick a preset (sphere, cone, torus, Möbius strip, surface of revolution) or type your own $\mathbf{r}(u,v)$. Hover any point to see the tangent vectors $\mathbf{r}_u$, $\mathbf{r}_v$, and the normal $\mathbf{r}_u \times \mathbf{r}_v$ rendered live, with an optional tangent plane overlay.
Surface Area
$\displaystyle A(S) = \iint_D |\mathbf{r}_u \times \mathbf{r}_v|\, dA$
Watch the Riemann sum $\sum |\mathbf{r}_u \times \mathbf{r}_v|\,\Delta u\,\Delta v$ converge to the surface area as you crank up the grid resolution. Heatmap mode colors the surface by the area magnifier $|\mathbf{r}_u \times \mathbf{r}_v|$ so you can see where the parameter grid stretches most.
Surface Integral
$\displaystyle \iint_S f\, dS = \iint_D f(\mathbf{r}(u,v))\,|\mathbf{r}_u \times \mathbf{r}_v|\, dA$
Pick any surface and any scalar field $f(x,y,z)$. The surface color-maps $f$ evaluated on $S$; the Riemann sum approximating $\iint_S f\, dS$ updates live as you refine the grid. Physical readings: mass of a thin shell, total charge, or average of $f$ over $S$.
